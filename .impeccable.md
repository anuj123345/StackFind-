## Design Context

### Users
Indian founders, developers, and solopreneurs building AI-powered products. They're budget-conscious, pragmatic, and frustrated by dollar-only pricing. They use this site on their laptop during work hours — light context. They know what they want; the job is discovery and vetting, not entertainment.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: editorial, trustworthy, founder-built.**
StackFind feels like a curated magazine for builders, not a flashy marketing site. Warm, opinionated, and India-proud without being garish.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Warm parchment background (`#FAF7F2`), white cards, warm near-black text.
- **Display font**: Bricolage Grotesque Variable — expressive, slightly eccentric, strong personality.
- **Body font**: Onest (via next/font/google) — humanist, clean, not on the reflex ban list.
- **Accent**: Indigo `#6366f1` for primary actions. Saffron `#D97706` for India/India-made markers.
- **Anti-references**: No glassmorphism for decoration, no gradient text, no cold dark mode, no neon accents.
- **References feel**: Claude.ai's warmth × editorial directory × Sirnik.co's asymmetric layouts.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn every pixel** — no decorative chrome that doesn't carry meaning.
2. **Editorial over dashboard** — stats and data as prose and inline proof, not metric tiles.
3. **India-proud, not India-loud** — 🇮🇳 markers and INR badges are precise signals, not decorations.
4. **Warm restraint** — warm palette used with 60-30-10 discipline; indigo accent is rare and intentional.
5. **Motion serves state** — animations on entrance and hover only; no ambient motion loops.

### Banned Patterns (already enforced)
- Gradient text (`background-clip: text` + linear-gradient) — use solid `#6366f1` instead
- Plus Jakarta Sans as body font — replaced with Onest
- 4-card hero metric grid — replaced with editorial proof strip
- Side-stripe `border-left > 1px` accents on cards
